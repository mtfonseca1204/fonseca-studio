MY ROLE

Product Designer: make on-chain creation feel like a consumer product

Caramel is a meme coin creation platform on LaChain, inspired by pump.fun. As Product Designer, I owned UX strategy, interface design, and branding for a hackathon-built product focused on clarity, speed, and accessibility.

Developed during BlockchainRio 2024 with developer Bruno Eleodo, the project combined product thinking, UX strategy, branding, and interface design into a lightweight launch experience.

UX strategy: Reduced cognitive load through simple step-by-step flows and clear interface patterns for first-time users.

Product design: Fast launch interactions inspired by consumer-grade UX, not developer tooling.

Branding: Playful, internet-native visual language structured enough to support product trust.

On-chain transparency: Smart contract interactions that abstract complexity without hiding how the system works.

Simplifying blockchain UX is not about removing depth. It's about removing unnecessary friction.

THE PROBLEM

Token launches still feel like developer tools

Launching tokens on-chain still feels overly technical for most users. Existing flows often require too many decisions, unfamiliar wallet interactions, and a level of blockchain knowledge that creates unnecessary friction.

For people interested in experimenting, building communities, or launching cultural internet-native assets, the process can feel intimidating instead of exciting.

The gap wasn't interest. It was the interface. Keep scrolling to see how we closed it.

THE SOLUTION

Frictionless creation without losing on-chain credibility

The goal was to reduce complexity, make the experience intuitive for non-technical users, create a fast flow from idea to launch, and balance ease of use with transparency around on-chain actions.

Caramel was designed as a frictionless token creation experience where users can create, launch, and interact with meme coins in just a few clicks:

  • Simple step-by-step flows to reduce cognitive load
  • Clear interface patterns to guide first-time users
  • Fast launch interactions inspired by consumer-grade UX
  • Transparent smart contract interactions that abstract complexity without hiding how the system works

Instead of overwhelming users with technical steps, Caramel turns token creation into a more approachable process while preserving the feeling of being truly on-chain.

Approachable, lightweight, community-driven. Structured enough to earn trust.

KEY DECISIONS

Three choices that shaped the product

Step-by-step over dashboard complexity

Instead of exposing every on-chain parameter at once, the flow guides users through one decision at a time. Reduced form complexity and guided interaction patterns kept first-time users moving forward.

Transparency without technical overload

Smart contract interactions were abstracted for clarity, but never hidden. Users understood what was happening on-chain without needing to parse raw contract data.

Playful branding, structured product

Meme culture informed the visual language, but the interface stayed clean and hierarchical. Approachable rather than intimidating, without losing credibility.

RESEARCH

Users wanted to launch. The tools weren't built for them.

The research process combined market analysis, competitive references, and quick user validation during the hackathon cycle.

The main insight was clear: users were interested in launching meme coins, but existing tools often felt too complex, confusing, or inaccessible for beginners. This reinforced the need for a product experience centered on clarity, trust, and immediate usability.

DESIGN

Lightweight interface, crypto-native energy

The design direction prioritized a lightweight and intuitive interface, influenced by products that reduce friction through strong visual hierarchy and minimal decision-making.

Intuitive onboarding

First-time users guided through each step with clear feedback and minimal jargon.

Reduced form complexity

Only essential fields visible at each stage. No parameter overload.

Guided interaction patterns

Consumer-grade UX patterns adapted for on-chain actions.

Internet-native branding

Playful visual language aligned with meme culture but structured for product trust.

WHAT I LEARNED

Lessons Learned

Simplifying blockchain UX is not about removing depth, but about removing unnecessary friction. Product design can translate complex infrastructure into experiences that feel fast, clear, and culturally relevant.

Full lessons from the project

Friction kills experimentation.

Users interested in meme coins weren't blocked by the idea. They were blocked by wallet interactions and unfamiliar flows.

Abstraction needs boundaries.

Hiding complexity entirely erodes trust. Showing enough to understand without overwhelming is the balance.

Hackathon speed forces clarity.

Limited time meant every screen had to earn its place. That constraint produced a sharper product than endless iteration would have.
