Problem

Desk operators spent 15 to 20 minutes hunting SLA breaches across spreadsheets and disconnected dashboards.

Solution

Single-screen platform: SLA status, liquidity trends, and history at a glance. Status first → data second → drill-down on demand

Result

61→88% task completion, ~30% faster time-to-insight. Impact below.

The situation

15 to 20 minutes to find a breach that costs money every second

When an SLA breaks, operators jump between Dune, spreadsheets, and manual reports. It takes 15 to 20 minutes to find the problem. Performance data was fragmented across tools with no single view to compare Market Makers side by side.

Every minute of delay costs money. Transparent.space needed to answer one question first: is this Market Maker healthy right now? Everything else could wait behind a deliberate drill-down.

How I worked

Founding design from zero, with PM and engineering

Joined before there was a product. Shaped IA, flows, and core UX from first wireframe to validated v2.

Benchmarked Dune, Bloomberg, Grafana, Datadog: users need the right data in the right order, not more data. v1 feedback: “I don’t know where to start.” Built 120+ modular components so design and dev shared one language.

What we built

One screen. Status first. Drill-down on demand.

One question drives the layout: “Is this Market Maker healthy right now?” Executives scan in seconds. Analysts get depth without losing their place.

01

Information Architecture

Two journeys (executive scan and analyst review) on one screen. Four layers: SLA Status, Liquidity Behavior, Market Comparison, Methodology.

02

Component Design

120+ modular components in Figma. Same card works as summary or detail. Light mode by default: clean, serious, built for high-stakes decisions.

03

Interaction Design

Summary view and expandable detail on every screen. Breadcrumbs show position. Alerts read fulfilled or not fulfilled, with trend data on expand.

04

Validation

v1 showed 12 metrics at once. We cut to 4 on the main view and moved the rest behind drill-down. Target: SLA diagnosis under 2 minutes.

Key choices

Three tradeoffs that shaped the experience

Binary SLA over percentage nuance

We considered showing uptime percentages front and center. Operators needed a yes/no answer first. We chose fulfilled or not fulfilled at the top, with full breakdown one level down.

Trend direction over static numbers

We considered absolute values as the hero metric. A $200K pool depth means nothing without direction. We chose rising, falling, or stable as the primary read.

4 metrics, not 12

We considered density to signal completeness. v1 overwhelmed users. We chose 4 critical metrics up front and hid the rest behind drill-down.

What changed

Validated with operators: early institutional traction

Usability sessions with 4 desk operators showed 61→88% task completion between v1 and v2. Cutting the primary view from 12 metrics to 4 delivered 40% faster time-to-status, moving SLA diagnosis toward the under-2-minute target we set during validation.

Early conversations with Kraken and Worldchain validated institutional traction. Outcomes were measured in operator sessions, not vanity metrics: users could scan health in seconds and drill into liquidity trends without losing their place.

Looking ahead

What I’d carry forward

In institutional products, restraint is the strategy. UI simplification without IA simplification just shifts confusion to a different screen.

Next: Predictive alerts before breaches fully hit; side-by-side comparison for up to 3 Market Makers.
