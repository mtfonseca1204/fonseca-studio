The situation

Remote care was necessary. The experience wasn’t ready.

40K+ beneficiaries needed telemedicine fast, but scheduling, insurance rules, and video consults landed on apps that felt opaque. Health urgency raises stress; every screen had to offer one clear next step.

The product served Brazil's largest healthcare cooperative during peak pandemic demand. Remote care was not optional, yet the existing experience treated insurance complexity and clinical urgency as separate problems instead of one anxious journey.

How I worked

Research first, then flows, with Karla Granadeiro

Surveys, interviews, and healthcare app benchmarks before any UI. Users wanted reassurance and clear next steps, not feature menus.

Delivered appointment flows, consultation interface, health history, and a neutral design system aligned with institutional trust.

What we built

One predictable path: sign in → schedule → consult → follow up

Four flows, one mental model. Each screen answers where the user is and what happens next.

  • Appointment: Symptom description through scheduling and confirmation
  • Consultation: Video and chat with medical professionals
  • History: Past consultations and recommendations in one place
  • Navigation: Low friction from sign-in to active consultation
Key choices

Three tradeoffs that shaped the experience

One primary action per screen

We considered a dashboard with multiple entry points. Research showed stressed users abandon when given options. Every screen guides toward a single next step.

Visual restraint over trend

Neutral palette, accessible type, functional icons. The UI had to feel institutional and calm, not like a consumer startup trying to “disrupt” healthcare.

Progress visibility in every flow

Users always know where they are: scheduling, waiting, in consultation, or follow-up. Consistent cues reduced drop-off in the scheduling funnel.

What changed

Shipped during COVID peak, validated through research, not vanity metrics

Shipped at COVID peak for 40K+ beneficiaries across Brazil's largest healthcare cooperative. Institutional rollout carried no published UX benchmarks, but guided flows showed clear gains: beneficiaries consulted remotely without clinic visits, and scheduling completion improved against the prior fragmented experience.

Research and usability testing surfaced consistent themes: easy, reassuring, predictable. Progress visibility in every flow reduced drop-off in the scheduling funnel, validating the one-path mental model we designed with Karla Granadeiro.

Looking ahead

What I’d carry forward

In health products, anxiety reduction is the engagement metric. Every ambiguous scheduling step added stress; every clear next step kept people in the flow.

Next: Real-time appointment status and integrated health records across consultations.
