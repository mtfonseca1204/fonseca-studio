MY ROLE

Brand Designer: craft a vegan bakery that feels real, not generic

NØRA Bakery is built around simplicity and care. Good food should be honest, well made, and inclusive. My role was to translate that philosophy into a complete brand identity and digital presence.

The work spanned visual identity, brand narrative, a landing page, and packaging direction. Every decision reinforced handcrafted quality, sensory warmth, and a timeless yet modern sensibility.

Visual identity: Logo, color palette, typography, and mood built around textures like linen, woodgrain, and natural light.

Brand narrative: Positioning that tells a story, not just sells pastries. Plant-based by design, inclusive by intent.

Digital presence: Landing page with emphasis on sensory experience, longer scroll depth, and newsletter engagement.

Packaging direction: Visual mood and system foundations for future physical applications.

The brand had to feel tactile and sincere online, not just represent a bakery from a distance.

THE PROBLEM

Another bakery wasn't an option

NØRA entered a competitive local market where many bakeries relied on generic visuals and uninspired positioning. The core challenge was to convey craft authenticity and premium experience through a visual language that feels both warm and elevated.

Digital touchpoints needed to extend the brand cohesively online while supporting future campaigns and community engagement. Overused bakery clichés would have undermined the vegan, Scandinavian-inspired positioning from the start.

Heritage cues plus modern clarity was the formula. Keep scrolling to see how it took shape.

THE SOLUTION

Craft-centric storytelling with sensory resonance

The project aimed to establish a differentiated brand identity, create a flexible visual system for physical and digital use, build an engaging online presence, and set a foundation for seasonal campaigns and newsletter growth.

The design strategy centered on craft-centric storytelling with a visual system that balances warmth and refined simplicity:

  • Logo and symbol: Custom typography combining subtle craft cues with modern restraint
  • Color palette: Earth tones and warm neutrals that evoke ingredients, oven glow, and artisanal materials
  • Typography: Elegant yet grounded type choices that feel readable and premium
  • Vibe and mood: Textures like linen, woodgrain, and flour dust under natural light

This identity system was designed to be flexible, memorable, and rooted in the emotional experience of artisan bakery.

Natural palettes, custom typography, and sensory photography styling. One consistent expression across every touchpoint.

KEY DECISIONS

Three choices that defined NØRA

Sensory storytelling over product shots alone

Competitors showed pastries. NØRA needed to evoke smell, taste, and feel through imagery and color. Sensory branding studies shaped every visual decision.

Heritage cues with modern restraint

Artisanal bakery culture informed the craft direction, but the typography and grid stayed clean and contemporary. Heritage plus modern clarity equals emotional credibility.

Digital that feels physical

The landing page was designed so the online experience felt like the physical brand, not a separate marketing layer. Texture, pacing, and narrative flow carried the same warmth.

RESEARCH

Understanding the gap in emotional storytelling

Brand and design decisions were informed by artisanal bakery culture, competitor analysis, sensory branding studies, and local audience expectations for premium food brands.

  • Artisanal bakery culture and craft food trends
  • Competitor analysis, identifying gaps in emotional storytelling
  • Sensory branding studies: how imagery and color evoke smell, taste, and feel
  • Local audience behavior and expectations for premium food brands

Key takeaway: heritage cues plus modern clarity creates emotional credibility.

WHAT I LEARNED

Lessons Learned

In premium food brands, emotional connection is as important as visual clarity. Sensory storytelling deepens engagement far beyond functional copy.

Full lessons from the project

Sensory storytelling deepens engagement.

Imagery and color that evoke taste and texture outperform product-only photography for premium food positioning.

Digital must feel like the physical brand.

Online presence can't just represent the bakery. It has to carry the same tactile sincerity.

Modularity pays off early.

Building for seasonal campaigns and newsletter growth from the start avoids redesign cycles later.
