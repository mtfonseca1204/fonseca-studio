MY ROLE

Telehealth for a workforce under stress

Petrobras Saúde is the corporate healthcare plan for Petrobras employees and their families, serving hundreds of thousands of beneficiaries across Brazil. The platform redesign focused on modernizing the digital experience during COVID-19 with user research and beneficiary needs at the center.

Working with Karla Granadeiro, I combined UX research, digital health workflows, and service design to deliver a trusted and accessible telehealth experience for a large institutional user base.

UX research: Telehealth usage trends, employee interviews, pain point mapping, and usability testing on prototype flows.

Workflow design: Symptom triage, remote consultation scheduling, and resource hub flows designed step by step.

Interface design: Straightforward language, modular screens, and reassuring visual cues for moments of health concern.

Visual system: Institutional trust aligned with Petrobras brand values, calm UI, and accessibility for diverse skill levels.

During a health crisis, clarity beats complexity. Every screen had to reduce stress, not add to it.

THE PROBLEM

Pandemic pressure on a system built for in-person care

When the pandemic began, traditional healthcare channels faced capacity challenges, risks of infection, and logistical barriers. Petrobras needed a solution that reduced pressure on physical clinics, allowed employees to access medical support remotely, and provided clear health guidance specific to COVID-19 symptoms and risk factors.

Existing health systems were often fragmented, intimidating, or unfriendly to users unfamiliar with remote care platforms. The platform had to feel reliable and trustworthy for a broad user audience, both medical and non-medical.

Users wanted reassurance, not complexity. Keep scrolling to see how the flows were structured.

THE SOLUTION

Clarity, empathy, and accessibility in every interaction

The goal was to enable remote access to medical professionals, provide clear direction for COVID-related concerns, reduce user stress in symptom evaluation, and work intuitively for users of all tech proficiency levels.

The Saúde Petrobras telemedicine experience was designed around four key components:

  • Symptom triage flow: Simple guided steps to help users describe health concerns and prioritize urgency
  • Remote consultation interface: Easy scheduling and live interaction with medical professionals
  • Resource and guidance hub: Centralized health information, FAQs, and personalized recommendations
  • Mobile-friendly experience: Support for on-the-go access during urgent needs

The user experience was intentionally simple so that even first-time telemedicine users could navigate without confusion. Clear language, progressive disclosure, and trust signals at every step.

Institutional goals included improved health outcomes and reduced clinic overload. The UX had to serve both.

KEY DECISIONS

Three choices that shaped the platform

Reassurance over medical jargon

Research revealed users wanted clear next steps, not medical jargon, and quick access without long waiting periods. Plain language and progressive disclosure replaced dense information screens.

Step-by-step triage, not dashboard overload

Modular screens guide users step by step through symptom evaluation. Minimal cognitive load at every interaction, especially critical during moments of health concern.

Institutional trust with human warmth

Visual system aligned with Petrobras brand values while staying calm and approachable. Soft contrasts, clear typography, and consistency across health flows to reduce user anxiety.

RESEARCH

RESULTS

Telemedicine that earned trust during a crisis

Impact and outcomes

Improved remote health access: Employees accessed care during critical pandemic periods without visiting clinics.

Positive ease-of-use feedback: Especially from first-time telemedicine users.

Reduced clinic burden: In-person services could focus on critical care.

WHAT I LEARNED

Lessons Learned

Clarity beats complexity, especially during health crises. Healthcare UX must reduce cognitive load while increasing confidence.

Full lessons from the project

Crisis UX demands simplicity.

When users are worried about their health, every extra decision point increases abandonment.

Trust signals are functional, not decorative.

Doctor availability, session confirmations, and secure UI cues matter as much as the consultation itself.

Institutional systems need human language.

Empathetic, human-centered copy bridges the gap between corporate healthcare and personal health anxiety.
